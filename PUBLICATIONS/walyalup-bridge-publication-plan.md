# Walyalup Bridge Publication Plan

The Walyalup Bridge Story is the first public entry point into the ecosystem. It must remain testimony first.

## Purpose

Publish the story in a dignified, accessible format that helps readers understand lived experience, structural failure, community care, and the possibility of practical civic change.

The story should invite action without turning hardship into marketing content.

## Page Structure

1. Title and subtitle
   - Clear title.
   - Short context line explaining that this is lived testimony and civic reflection.

2. Reader context note
   - Explain that the story includes homelessness, survival, grief, systems failure, and community care.
   - Keep the note brief and human.

3. Author attribution
   - Kristal Cornwell as author.
   - Note AI/editorial support only if relevant and approved.

4. Story body
   - Preserve section pacing and emotional rhythm.
   - Use generous line spacing, narrow readable measure, and mobile-first typography.
   - Do not add sensational pull quotes from traumatic passages.

5. Support prompts
   - Place prompts at the beginning, after major sections, and at the end.
   - Use language such as: "If this story moved you, support practical care in Fremantle through approved pathways." 
   - Do not interrupt the most vulnerable sections with conversion prompts.

6. Community of Kindness explanation
   - Publish only after approval is confirmed.
   - Explain the community work plainly.
   - State recipient, fund handling, reporting, contact, and accountability.

7. Author note and books
   - Include author note after the story.
   - Mention related books or creative works as context, not as the primary conversion goal.

8. Share campaign
   - Provide simple share text.
   - Encourage readers to share with care, dignity, and context.

## Donation and Support Language

Use direct, transparent language:

- "Support practical community care."
- "Help provide meals, connection, dignity, and local support where approved pathways are confirmed."
- "Register to volunteer, contribute goods, or receive updates."

Avoid:

- pity hooks
- rescue language
- exaggerated claims
- unapproved partnership language
- implying all donations are authorised before confirmation

## Editorial Guardrails

- Preserve Kristal's voice.
- Improve readability only where it does not alter meaning.
- Do not fabricate dialogue, dates, events, endorsements, or approvals.
- Keep civic critique intelligible and intact.
- Classify public claims correctly.

## Success Measures

Measure more than money:

- story reads
- meaningful shares
- approved donations or meals supported
- volunteer interest
- goods/resource offers
- stakeholder conversations
- Living Layers registrations
- TimeShift Freo expressions of interest
- qualitative feedback showing trust, dignity, and understanding
