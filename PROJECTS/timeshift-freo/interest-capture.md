# TimeShift Freo Interest Capture

TimeShift Freo is the future-facing civic, cultural, creative, and place-based digital experience pathway. In the first launch, it is an interest capture page, not a finished app.

## Purpose

Introduce the concept clearly and invite early supporters, collaborators, funders, artists, historians, residents, businesses, community groups, and technical contributors.

The page should show how the Walyalup Bridge Story could eventually become one living narrative layer inside a wider place-based memory and civic imagination ecosystem.

## Public Positioning

Plain-language concept:

> TimeShift Freo explores how Fremantle/Walyalup stories, places, memories, art, and community knowledge could become living digital layers that people can discover, contribute to, and care for over time.

## Interest Categories

- Resident or local community member.
- Artist, musician, writer, performer, or creative worker.
- Historian, archivist, researcher, or cultural worker.
- Local business, venue, or tourism operator.
- Community organisation or service provider.
- Council, civic, grant, or policy stakeholder.
- Developer, designer, AR/VR, mapping, data, or AI contributor.
- Funder, sponsor, or partner.
- Curious supporter.

## Minimum Form Fields

Required:

- name or organisation name
- email
- interest category
- consent checkbox

Optional:

- role or relationship to Fremantle/Walyalup
- collaboration idea
- skills, resources, venue, funding, or story interest
- permission to be contacted
- notes, with privacy reminder

## Guardrails

- Do not imply the AR platform is already built.
- Do not imply council, cultural, First Nations, artist, or community endorsement unless documented.
- Do not collect cultural knowledge, images, or stories for public use without a specific consent pathway.
- Keep deeper OmniStrux/CareX/TideCoin concepts out of the first mainstream explanation unless the reader opts into deeper architecture.

## Success Criteria

- Clear early interest list.
- Identified collaborators and stakeholder categories.
- No overclaiming of readiness, partnership, or technical completion.
- Strong bridge from Walyalup Bridge Story to future place-based storytelling.
