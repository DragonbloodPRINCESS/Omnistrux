# Living Layers MVP

Living Layers begins as a consent-first registration and participation capture page. It is not a full application in the first launch.

## Purpose

Let people safely register their relationship to the community and their desired level of involvement without forcing sensitive disclosure.

Living Layers should begin mapping people, needs, skills, stories, resources, places, support pathways, and opportunities for connection only when doing so is transparent, voluntary, and useful.

## Initial Pathways

- I need support.
- I want to volunteer.
- I can donate goods, meals, time, or resources.
- I am a local business or community organisation.
- I have lived experience and may want my voice included safely.
- I am interested in civic design or future projects.
- I want updates only.

## Minimum Form Fields

Required:

- name or preferred name
- email or preferred contact method
- pathway category
- consent checkbox acknowledging the form purpose

Optional:

- phone
- suburb or relationship to Fremantle/Walyalup
- skills, resources, support interest, or collaboration type
- privacy preference
- permission to follow up
- notes, with a reminder not to include sensitive details unless necessary

## Consent Wording

Plain-language example:

> This form collects only the information needed to understand how you may want to participate or stay informed. Your response will not be published without permission. You can share as little as you like. Please avoid including sensitive personal details unless needed for follow-up. You can ask for your information to be corrected or removed.

## Safety Rules

- Do not collect detailed hardship stories in the first MVP.
- Do not promise services that do not exist.
- Do not expose submissions publicly.
- Do not use responses for marketing without explicit permission.
- Export data in a portable format.

## MVP Success Criteria

- People can register interest without confusion.
- The form explains consent, privacy, access, and withdrawal.
- Submissions can be exported.
- The data collected is useful and minimal.
- No one is required to disclose trauma, identity, location, or hardship details to participate.
